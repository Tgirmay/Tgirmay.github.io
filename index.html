Over the years, many people have claimed that the number of violent crimes in the United States has been a result of the increase in violent video games. Over the last couple decades, many news outlets headline articles about a criminal who committed a very violent crime being "obsessed with violent video games" and that being the reason for thier actions. Other studies have been conducted to find see if playing violent video games and violent behavoiours or correlations between the two. This debate has devolved into some groups blaming video games as a whole and not just the violent genres. Though there have been many studies and papers published disproving this thought, many people still beleive a correlation exists. The debate continues to this day and important people such as President Trump blamed video games for a school shooting in 2018. Critics to this debate say that if they are to blame, countries who have a high game-playing population should have a high crime rate. This tutorial aims to see how the number of violent crimes compares with the sales of violent video games. If the rise of these games cause violence, there should be a significant correlation between the two.

I will be using the Video Game Sales dataset found on Kaggle and view the data on sales of games of the genre "Shooter", for this is considered by many as the most violent genre because games in this category depict the player or other people doing these violent crimes. I will compare this dataset with the State Crime Dataset fron the CORGIS Dataset Project. According to a computational formulas sheet posted by the Office of the Attorney General, "A crime rate is calculated by dividing the number of reported crimes by the total population."

Conclusion¶

From the scatterplots above, we can see that there is a relatively high negative correlation between the total violent crime rate and both shooters and action games, and that the correlation with fighting games, while low, is also negative. Furthermore, the p values for the correlations with shooters and action games are very low so for a hypothesis test, we can reject the null hypothesis.

This does not mean that video games reduce violence for correlation does not cause causation; however, for video games to be the cause violence, there needs to be a positive correaltion between the sales and crime rate. Since we did not see this trend in the data, we can say that video games are not to blame for violent crimes. This debate needs to be put to rest so that we can focus on finding real causes of violence so we can reduce our crime rates further.
